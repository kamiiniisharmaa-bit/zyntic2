Our story

barca was born from a simple question: why should beautiful design come at the cost of durability or ethics? In 2020, our founders — a ceramicist and an industrial designer — set out to create a brand that bridges timeless aesthetics with responsible production. We started with a single vase, hand-thrown in a small studio in San Francisco. Today, we collaborate with artisans and sustainable manufacturers across the globe.

Every piece you see is the result of countless hours of refinement, material research, and a deep respect for slow living. We don't do trends — we create quiet, essential pieces that grow more meaningful with use. Our name, barca, echoes 'synthetic' and 'authentic' — a reminder that modern materials can coexist with natural honesty.

From our ceramic collection to the oak and steel lighting, each product is designed to be kept, repaired, and passed on. We're proud to be a climate-neutral committed brand, and we're just getting started.

From the workshop

Every barca piece passes through hands that care. Our ceramic collection is thrown on potter's wheels in small batches, then kiln-fired for durability. Our wooden trays are hand-sanded and finished with natural oil, celebrating grain patterns instead of hiding them.

We visit our partner studios twice a year to ensure quality and respect. This is slow manufacturing in a world of fast choices — and we wouldn't have it any other way. The imperfections you might notice (a slight variation in glaze, a unique wood knot) are not flaws; they’re signatures of authenticity.

We also believe in radical transparency: every product page lists the origin, materials, and care instructions. No greenwashing, no hidden fine print. Just objects you can trust.
